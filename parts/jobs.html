Jobs on the CryptPad team

CryptPad is developed by XWiki SAS, a French software company that has been producing open-source code since 2004.

Limitations

A portion of our funding comes from European and French grants. Funding from these sources can only be spent on salaries in the relevant jurisdiction. For this reason we will prefer candidates from France or other European Union member states.

We are able to provide support for immigration to very promising candidates from beyond the EU.

Perks

We strive to provide an environment that is conducive to a good work/life balance. This includes a 7 hour work day, 25 vacation days per year, and a 4.5 day work week.

We have offices in Paris, France and Iaşi, Romania with subsidies for public transit or cycling. For those that prefer partial or fully remote work, a monthly budget for home office accommodations is available.

As a small company, we aim to help our team members grow and prefer to promote current team members into leadership roles. You have the opportunity to travel throughout Europe at the company's expense when promoting our projects at conferences.

Team members that have an interest in continuing an academic background are welcome to publish papers to share the results of our latest research with the wider community.

What we look for

  • Experience with JavaScript and NodeJS
  • Experience/interest in digital privacy issues
  • Experience/interest in web-browser ttechnologies
  • Familiarity with open-source software development
  • Good communication skills
  • English fluency, one additional spoken/written language preferred
  • Nice to have: PhD graduate in a related field

How to apply

Send an email to jobs@xwiki.com with a brief introduction and a CV/Resumé attached. Include the job title in the subject line so we don't miss it.

Roles

Web developer

We're seeking a talented front-end developer with a focus on web application usability

  • Front-end development with (vanilla) JavaScript technologies
  • Help design and implement intuitive privacy controls and productivity-enhancing features
  • Improve platform accessibility for all users
  • Work with modern web APIs like SharedWorkers, ServiceWorkers, IndexedDB, and WebAssembly

Full stack developer

responsibilities

  • Linux server deployment and administration
  • Overseeing our release process: code review, quality assurance, testing
  • Participation in research projects, designing architecture for new features on the server and client

Instance admin

responsibilities

  • Non-technical instance administration
  • Answer support tickets and email
  • Provide assistance with billing issues
  • Monitor social media and make announcements

Office technology specialist

responsibilities

  • Help stabilize our recently introduced office editors
  • Debug and improve WebAssembly file format converters
  • Adhere to strict web application security practices
